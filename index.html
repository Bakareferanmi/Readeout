The short version: Drop in a CSV or JSON file, pick columns, and Readeout builds summary statistics and charts instantly — all in your browser, nothing uploaded.

Quick start

Drag a file onto the drop zone, or use Browse to pick one from disk. Once loaded, Readeout profiles every column (type, min/max, mean, distinct count, and so on) and shows them in the readout panel above the raw data table.

Pick an X column (and a Y column where the chart type needs one), choose a chart type from the dropdown, and the chart renders live. Tick the insight checkboxes under the chart to choose which auto-generated observations travel with a PNG/PDF export.

Supported files

.csvComma-separated values with a header row. Column types are auto-detected (number, date, text, category).
.jsonAn array of flat objects, each one treated as a row. Nested fields are not parsed.

Max file size is 25MB. Everything is parsed client-side — larger files simply take longer, they aren't rejected for content reasons.

Chart interactions

Beyond picking a type and axes, a few controls above the chart change how it renders or behaves:

  • 3D — adds a pseudo-3D depth style to bar, histogram, pie, donut, stacked bar, overlapping bar, and gauge charts. Switching to a chart type that doesn't support it turns it back off automatically.
  • Trend — overlays a moving-average trend line on line and area charts.
  • Goal — on a gauge chart, sets the target value the gauge is measured against.
  • Zoom & pan — on charts with a continuous axis (bar, line, area, histogram, stacked, overlap, scatter, bubble), scroll or pinch to zoom, hold Ctrl/Cmd and drag to pan, and double-click or use Reset zoom to return to the default view.
  • Click to filter — clicking a bar or segment on bar, stacked bar, overlapping bar, or histogram charts filters the data table below to just that group; click the filter chip to clear it.

Dashboard

Switch to the Dashboard tab to pin multiple charts side by side instead of viewing one at a time. Tap + Add chart to create a card, then set its type and columns independently of the single-chart view. Cards and their settings are saved automatically in this browser, so the dashboard is still there next time you open Readeout with the same session.

Exporting

Every chart — single view or dashboard card — can be exported as a PNG or a PDF, with the ticked insight lines included as a caption underneath. The whole dashboard can also be exported as one composite image or PDF via the buttons above the dashboard grid.

  • Background color — pick any export background from the swatches or the color picker; chart text and gridlines automatically switch between light and dark styling to stay readable.
  • Export folder — optionally choose a dedicated folder (where your browser supports it) so exports stop landing in the default Downloads folder.
  • Resolution — exports are rendered at a higher pixel density than what's on screen, so PNGs and PDFs stay sharp even when zoomed in or printed.

Session history

Readeout keeps a rolling history of recent sessions (up to 15) so you can pick up where you left off. Open it with the History button. Smaller datasets are stored in full; larger ones are kept as metadata only (column names and stats, not every row) to stay within browser storage limits. History lives entirely in localStorage on this device — clearing your browser data clears it too.

Data & privacy

Readeout has no backend. Files you load are read and processed entirely in your browser; nothing is uploaded, transmitted, or seen by anyone else. See the full Privacy Policy for details.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

The short version: Readeout runs entirely in your browser. Any file you open is read and processed locally on your device. We do not upload, transmit, store, or have access to your data — ever.

What Readeout Does With Your Data

When you drop a file into Readeout, it is read directly by your browser using standard client-side file APIs. Parsing, charting, and every calculation you see happen locally, on your own machine. At no point is the file's contents sent to Readeout's servers or to any third party, because Readeout does not have a server that receives your data.

If you close the tab or refresh the page, the data is gone — Readeout does not persist your files anywhere.

Information We Collect

Readeout itself collects no personal data and has no account system, login, or tracking of file contents. Depending on how you access the page, standard web infrastructure may log limited technical information, such as:

  • Basic request metadata (e.g. IP address, browser type, timestamp) that any web server or CDN logs by default.
  • Aggregated, anonymous usage analytics, if enabled, used only to understand overall traffic — never tied to the content of any file you analyze.

We do not sell data, run advertising trackers, or share information with data brokers.

Cookies & Local Storage

Readeout may use your browser's local storage to remember interface preferences, such as your last-used chart colors or layout settings. This information stays on your device and is never transmitted anywhere.

Third-Party Services

Readeout loads a small number of third-party libraries (such as charting and font libraries) from public content delivery networks so the app can render properly. These services may receive standard network request metadata (like your IP address) as an inherent part of serving files over the web, but they do not receive your uploaded data.

Children's Privacy

Readeout is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.
